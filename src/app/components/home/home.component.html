Simon Bernard

Associate Professor (Maître de Conférence) @ LITIS lab., University Rouen Normandy

I am an associate professor (MCF) at the University of Rouen Normandy (URN), and a member of the Machine Learning research team of the LITIS lab. (UR 4108). I am also a member of the Data, Learning, Knowledge research group in the CNRS Research Federation NormaSTIC (FR 3638).

My research focuses on machine learning, and more specifically on ensemble methods and deep learning methods. More recently, I've taken a keen interest in machine learning for physics, particularly with operator learning methods and/or physic-informed learning methods. Other learning problems I have recently tackled include multi-view learning, weakly supervised learning and anomaly detection.

I teach computer science and machine learning at the Faculty of Science et Techniques of the University of Rouen Normandy, to bachelor and master students. Today, I mostly teach web programming and the theoretical and methodological foundations of machine learning to students of the SID master's program.

Other responsibilities:

  • Member of the Research Committee and the Academic Council of the University of Rouen Normandy
  • Member of the Physics department council of the University of Rouen Normandy
  • In charge of the second year of the Electronic, Electric Energy, Automatic (EEEA) Licence program at the University of Rouen Normandie
